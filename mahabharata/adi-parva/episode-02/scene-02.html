Ādi Parva · Episode 2 · Scene 2

Āruṇi

Āruṇi — The Living Embankment

Āruṇi — The Living Embankment

The next evening…

Neelakrishna could hardly wait.

“Appaa…
what next?
Any more surprise upākhyānas?”

Niveditaa smiled.

“I’m sure there are.”

Appaa’s eyes twinkled.

“You’re right.
This time we meet a great ṛṣi named Āpoda Dhaumya.
And within his story…
lie three more—
one for each of his three students.”

Neelakrishna’s face brightened.

“Ah…
stories within stories.
I like that.”

After a moment he asked,

“Then…
who do we meet first, Appaa?”

“Āruṇi.”

The name rested quietly between them.

“What happened to him?”

Appaa began.

“Āruṇi lived in the āśrama of the great ṛṣi Dhaumya.
Like the other students there, his days flowed between study and service.
He learnt the Vedas.
He served his guru.
He shared in the work of the āśrama.
Then, one day, Dhaumya called him.
‘Āruṇi…
the embankment of one of our fields has broken.
Go…
and mend it.’”

Āruṇi bowed to his guru and set out without delay.

Before long, he reached the field.

The embankment had broken.

Water was steadily flowing through the breach, spreading into the field beyond.

Āruṇi gathered wet earth and packed it into the opening.

For a few moments, the water slowed.

Then, little by little, it found its way through again.

He tried once more.

The earth gave way again.

Patiently, he rebuilt the embankment.

Again and again, the water broke through.

Again and again, he tried.

At last he paused.

The field was quiet.

Only the sound of flowing water remained.

Āruṇi stood there, thinking.

Then a thought came to him.

“If the embankment will not hold…
perhaps I can.”

Without another moment’s hesitation,

Āruṇi lay down across the breach.

The cold water pressed against him.

The earth gathered around his body.

Little by little,

the flowing water came to a halt.

The field grew still.

And Āruṇi remained there…

quietly…

holding the embankment in place.

Time passed.

The sun slowly moved across the sky.

Back at the āśrama,

the day’s work went on.

As evening drew near,

Dhaumya looked around.

“Where is Āruṇi?”

The other students replied,

“Bhagavan…
you sent him to mend the embankment.”

Dhaumya rose at once.

“Come.
Let us go and look for him.”

Together,

the guru and his students made their way towards the fields.

When they arrived,

Dhaumya called out,

“Āruṇi!
Where are you, my child?”

His voice carried across the quiet fields.

For a moment,

there was no reply.

Then,

from somewhere ahead,

a voice answered,

“Bhagavan…
I am here.”

The voice came from the embankment itself.

The moment he heard his guru’s voice,

Āruṇi rose at once.

As he stepped away,

the embankment gave way once more.

Water rushed through the breach again.

Āruṇi hastened to his guru,

bowed low,

and stood before him.

“Bhagavan…
the water kept breaking through the embankment.
I could find no other way to stop it.
So I lay across the breach.
The moment I heard your voice…
I came.”

For a few moments,

Dhaumya stood without speaking.

His disciple’s clothes were drenched.

Wet earth still clung to his body.

Behind him,

the water flowed quietly once more through the broken embankment.

But Dhaumya’s eyes rested only on Āruṇi.

Then he stepped forward

and gently placed his hand upon his head.

“Āruṇi…
today,
you have truly carried out my word.
Because you rose by breaking open the embankment,
from this day,
you shall be known as Uddālaka.
And because you fulfilled my word with such steadfastness,
you shall attain śreyas—
the highest good.
The Vedas shall shine forth within you.
And all the teachings of Dharma
shall become clear to you.”

Silence settled gently between them.

At last,

Appaa looked at Neelakrishna.

“What is it, Neelu?
Was it Āruṇi’s courage that impressed you?”

Neelakrishna slowly shook his head.

“No…
not just that.”

For a while,

he remained quiet.

“When the embankment kept breaking…
he didn’t stop trying.
Again…
and again…
nothing worked.
His body must have been tired.
His mind too.”

He paused.

“But somehow…
even then…
he could still see an idea.
And once he saw it…
he simply did it.”

Neither of them spoke.

After a while,

Niveditaa said softly,

“Appaa…
Āruṇi held his own body against the water…
so the water would not flow away.”

She paused.

“And Dhaumya blessed him…
that in the same way…
he would hold the Vedas…
and the teachings of Dharma.”

Appaa looked at Niveditaa.

A quiet smile spread across his face.

He said nothing.
