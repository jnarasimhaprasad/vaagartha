Ādi Parva · Episode 1 · Scene 6

The Tree of Dharma

The Tree of Dharma

The Tree of Dharma

The next evening…
the children gathered beside Appaa once again.

Neelakrishna could hardly wait.

“Appaa…
we’re waiting for the second tree!”

Niveditaa smiled.

“Yes…
I’m curious to see…
what Vyāsa shows us this time.”

Appaa looked at both of them.

“Then…
listen carefully.”

“Yesterday…
Vyāsa showed us…
a tree that had grown from manyu.”

He paused.

“Today…
he shows us another tree.”

“Not because the Pāṇḍavas never felt anger…
or sorrow…
or disappointment.
They did.
But something deeper…
guided their lives.”

Appaa recited,

युधिष्ठिरो धर्ममयो महाद्रुमः
स्कन्धोऽर्जुनो भीमसेनोऽस्य शाखाः ।
माद्रीसुतौ पुष्पफले समृद्धे
मूलं कृष्णो ब्रह्म च ब्राह्मणाश्च ॥

He continued,

“Yudhiṣṭhira is the great tree of Dharma.
Arjuna is its trunk.
Bhīmasena is its branches.
The sons of Mādrī—
Nakula and Sahadeva—
are its flowers and fruits.
Its roots are…
Kṛṣṇa…
the Vedas…
and the Brāhmaṇas.”

Neelakrishna interrupted.

“Wait…
the roots.
Last time…
there was only one.”

Niveditaa nodded.

“And this time…
there are three.”

Appaa smiled.

“Yes.
Kṛṣṇa…
the Vedas…
and the Brāhmaṇas.”

Neelakrishna looked puzzled.

“Why three, Appaa?”

“Because each one nourishes dharma in a different way.”

“The Vedas are the words of the ancient ṛṣis.
They show us what dharma is.”

“The Brāhmaṇas preserve those words…
study them…
live by them…
and pass them on to the next generation.”

He paused.

“And Kṛṣṇa…
whenever dharma is in danger…
it never stands alone.
Someone always rises…
to protect it.
In this story…
that protector is Kṛṣṇa.”

Neelakrishna was quiet for a while.

“Appaa…
why does dharma need so much care?”

Appaa smiled.

“Tell me…
why do you take so much care of your body?”

Neelakrishna looked surprised.

“My body?”

“Yes.
Why do you eat good food…
exercise…
bathe…
and get enough sleep?”

“So I stay healthy,”
said Neelakrishna.

“And why does that matter?”

Niveditaa answered,

“Then…
we take care of dharma…
because…
it becomes the strength…
that takes care of us.”

Appaa nodded.

“And so…
Yudhiṣṭhira stands as the great tree of Dharma…
nourished…
and held firm…
by those roots.
Arjuna…
its trunk.
Bhīma…
its branches.
Nakula and Sahadeva…
its flowers and fruits.”

The room was quiet.

After a while, Niveditaa spoke softly.

“Appaa…
when you first recited the śloka…
I was trying to remember…
Yudhiṣṭhira the tree…
Arjuna the trunk…
Bhīma the branches…
Nakula and Sahadeva the flowers and fruits…”

She paused.

“But now…
I can’t seem to separate them anymore.
I only see…
one great tree.”

Appaa looked at her for a long moment.

“Very good.”

His voice was quiet.

“You’ve begun to see the tree.”
