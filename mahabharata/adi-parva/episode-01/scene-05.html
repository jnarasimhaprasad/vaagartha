Vyāsa · Gaṇeśa

The Tree of Manyu

The Tree of Manyu

← Previous scene Next scene — in preparation

Neelakrishna could hardly contain himself.

“So… the story has finally begun!”

“We’ll finally get to meet… Bhīṣma… Droṇa… Kṛṣṇa… the Kauravas… and the Pāṇḍavas!”

“Yes,” said Appaa. “Very soon.”

“Wait…” said Niveditaa. “We already know the Kauravas and the Pāṇḍavas stood on opposite sides. But… how did they become so different?”

“That,” Appaa said, “is the very first thing Sauti wanted the sages to understand. Tell me… suppose someone paints beautifully… or sings wonderfully… or comes first in the exam. What should happen inside us?”

“We should be happy for them,” said Neelakrishna.

“And appreciate them,” added Niveditaa.

“Yes. That is what should happen. But does it always happen?”

Neither of them answered immediately.

“Not always…” said Niveditaa. “Sometimes… we feel a little uncomfortable.”

“But... why...?” asked Neelakrishna.

“Because… instead of simply admiring them… we quietly place ourselves beside them. We begin measuring ourselves against them. And then… in our own eyes… we begin to feel smaller.”

Niveditaa was silent for a while.

“So… the same goodness… can make me happy… or unhappy. But… the goodness itself hasn’t changed.”

“Yes. And if we keep feeding that feeling… the very person we should have admired… begins to trouble us. Our ṛṣis had a beautiful Sanskrit word for that whole movement of the mind. Manyu.”

The word rested quietly in the room.

“Before Sauti introduced the Kauravas and the Pāṇḍavas… he first showed the sages… what was shaping their lives.”

Then he recited,

दुर्योधनो मन्युमयो महाद्रुमः कर्णः स्कन्धः शकुनिस्तस्य शाखाः ।
दुःशासनः पुष्पफले समृद्धे मूलं राजा धृतराष्ट्रोऽमनीषी ॥

(Duryodhana is a tree of manyu; Karṇa its trunk, Śakuni its branches, Duḥśāsana its flowers and fruit, and the root is Dhṛtarāṣṭra, the amanīṣī.)

“So… Duryodhana himself… is this tree.”

“Yes. Look carefully. Vyāsa has placed each one exactly where he belongs. Let’s see why. Suppose, for a moment, Vyāsa had written— कर्णः मूलम्. Would that have felt right?”

“I’m not sure…” said Neelakrishna.

“I don’t think so,” said Niveditaa.

“Why?”

“Because… Duryodhana had already begun hating the Pāṇḍavas when they were children. Karṇa wasn’t even there.”

“Exactly. The root must already be there before the tree begins to grow.”

Appaa pointed to the next word. स्कन्धः

“What does a trunk do?”

“It holds the tree,” said Neelakrishna.

“And carries all its weight,” added Niveditaa.

“Yes. Again and again… Duryodhana was shown another path. Bhīṣma spoke. Droṇa advised. Vidura counselled. In the end… even Kṛṣṇa himself came, with one last chance for peace. But there was one man who never left Duryodhana’s side.”

“Karṇa,” said Neelakrishna.

“The trunk doesn’t decide where the tree should grow. But it bears the tree wherever it chooses to grow. Could Duryodhana have continued standing against the Pāṇḍavas without Karṇa beside him?”

Neelakrishna slowly shook his head. “He would have been standing alone.”

“That is why Vyāsa made Karṇa the trunk.”

Appaa pointed to the next word. शाखाः

“What do branches do?”

“They spread,” said Neelakrishna.

“And they reach places the trunk never can,” added Niveditaa.

“Who reached the Pāṇḍavas… not with arrows… but with dice?”

“Śakuni,” said Neelakrishna.

“That is why Vyāsa made him the branches.”

Appaa moved to the next line. पुष्पफले

“What was the moment when the whole world saw how far the Kauravas had fallen?”

The children became quiet.

“Draupadī’s humiliation…” said Niveditaa.

“And who laid hands on her?”

“Duḥśāsana,” said Neelakrishna.

“Yes. The humiliation of Draupadī was not where this tree began. It was what the tree had finally come to. That is why Vyāsa made Duḥśāsana its flowers and fruits.”

Finally, Appaa pointed to the last line. मूलं राजा धृतराष्ट्रोऽमनीषी

“The root…” said Neelakrishna. “The part we never see.”

“But the part without which the whole tree cannot stand,” added Niveditaa.

“Exactly. Hidden… yet holding everything above it. That is why Vyāsa made Dhṛtarāṣṭra the root.”

Niveditaa looked again at the last word. अमनीषी

“Appaa… what does amanīṣī mean?”

Appaa pointed to the verse again.

“You remember? Bhīṣma spoke. Vidura spoke. Kṛṣṇa spoke. Dhṛtarāṣṭra understood every one of them. Yet his mind would not go where his understanding led.”

“Vyāsa called such a man… amanīṣī.”

The room became quiet again.

No one looked at the verse anymore.

The tree of Manyu stood before them.

“Vyāsa called such a man… amanīṣī.”

After a while, Neelakrishna broke the silence.

“Appaa…
what about the Pāṇḍavas?”

“Yes…”

said Niveditaa.

“I’m sure Vyāsa didn’t leave us with just one tree.”

Appaa nodded.

“No…
he didn’t.”

“He showed us…
the tree of dharma too.”

Neelakrishna’s eyes lit up.

“Ah…
there’s another tree!”

Niveditaa’s face softened.

“Then…
there is another way to live.”

Appaa looked at both of them with quiet satisfaction.

“Yes.
That’s where we’ll begin tomorrow.”
