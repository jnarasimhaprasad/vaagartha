Ādi Parva · Episode 1 · Scene 5

Vyāsa · Gaṇeśa

The Tree of Manyu

The Tree of Manyu

← Vyāsa Meets Gaṇeśa The Tree of Dharma →

Neelakrishna could hardly contain himself.

“So… the story has finally begun!”

“We’ll finally get to meet… Bhīṣma… Droṇa… Kṛṣṇa… the Kauravas… and the Pāṇḍavas!”

“Yes,” said Appaa. “Very soon.”

“Wait…” said Niveditaa. “We already know the Kauravas and the Pāṇḍavas stood on opposite sides. But… how did they become so different?”

“That,” Appaa said, “is the very first thing Sauti wanted the sages to see.”

“Tell me… suppose someone paints beautifully… or sings wonderfully… or comes first in the exam. What should happen inside us?”

“We should be happy for them,” said Neelakrishna.

“And appreciate them,” added Niveditaa.

“Yes. That is what should happen. But does it always happen?”

Neither of them answered immediately.

“Not always…”

said Niveditaa.

“Sometimes…
we feel a little uncomfortable…”

She hesitated.

“…maybe…
even jealous.”

“Appaa…”

said Neelakrishna,

“when my friend wins a game…
I do feel bad for a while.”

“But…
why should that happen?”

“Because…”

Appaa said gently,

“instead of simply admiring them…
we quietly place ourselves beside them.
We begin measuring ourselves against them.
And then…
in our own eyes…
we begin to feel smaller.”

“Appaa…”

said Neelakrishna,

“is that why Duryodhana could never admire the Pāṇḍavas?”

Niveditaa was quiet for a moment.

“So…
the same goodness…
can make me happy…
or unhappy.
The goodness never changed…
only the way I look at it changed.”

Appaa nodded.

“Yes.
And if we keep feeding that feeling…
the very person we should have admired…
begins to trouble us.
Our ṛṣis had a beautiful Sanskrit word for that whole movement of the mind.
Manyu.”

The word rested quietly in the room.

“Before Sauti introduced the Kauravas and the Pāṇḍavas…
he first showed the sages…
what was shaping their lives.”

Appaa recited,

दुर्योधनो मन्युमयो महाद्रुमः
कर्णः स्कन्धः शकुनिस्तस्य शाखाः ।
दुःशासनः पुष्पफले समृद्धे
मूलं राजा धृतराष्ट्रोऽमनीषी ॥

Appaa continued…

“It means—
Duryodhana is a great tree of manyu;
Karṇa its trunk,
Śakuni its branches,
Duḥśāsana its flowers and fruits,
and the root is Dhṛtarāṣṭra…
the amanīṣī.”

Neelakrishna’s eyes widened.

“That must be a huge tree, Appaa!”

“But…
why did Vyāsa place Karṇa as the trunk…
Śakuni as the branches…
Duḥśāsana as the flowers…
and Dhṛtarāṣṭra as the root?”

Niveditaa looked thoughtful.

“Surely…
every place must mean something.”

“Yes,” said Appaa.

“Look carefully.
Vyāsa has placed each one exactly where he belongs.
Let’s see why.”

“Suppose, for a moment…
Vyāsa had written—
कर्णः मूलम्
Karṇa, the root.
Would that have felt right?”

“I’m not sure…”

said Neelakrishna.

“I don’t think so,”

said Niveditaa.

“Why?” asked Appaa

“Because…
Duryodhana had already begun hating the Pāṇḍavas when they were children.
Karṇa wasn’t even there.”

“Exactly.
A root has to be there…
before the tree begins to grow.”

Appaa pointed to the next word.

स्कन्धः

“Vyāsa says Karṇa is the skandha—
the trunk.”

“What does a trunk do?”

“It holds the tree,”

said Neelakrishna.

“And carries all its weight,”

added Niveditaa.

“Yes.
There were many people around Duryodhana…
who disagreed with him…
who warned him…
who tried to show him another path.
Bhīṣma rebuked him.
Droṇa advised him.
Vidura counselled him.
In the end…
even Kṛṣṇa himself came…
with one last chance for peace.
But there was one man…
who never stopped standing beside Duryodhana.”

“Karṇa,”

said Neelakrishna.

“Yes.
The trunk doesn’t decide where the tree should grow.
But it bears the whole tree…
wherever it chooses to grow.”

“Could Duryodhana have continued standing against the Pāṇḍavas…
without Karṇa beside him?”

Neelakrishna slowly shook his head.

“He would have been standing alone.”

“That…
is why Vyāsa made Karṇa the trunk.”

Appaa pointed to the next word.

शाखाः

“Tell me…
what do branches do?”

“They spread,”

said Neelakrishna.

“And they reach places…
the trunk never can,”

added Niveditaa.

Appaa nodded.

“Now think…
who struck the Pāṇḍavas the hardest?”

“Karṇa,”

said Neelakrishna.

Appaa asked: “With what?”

Neelakrishna replied:

“His arrows.”

“And Duryodhana?”

“His gadā.”

“Droṇa?”

“His weapons.”

Appaa gently shook his head.

“No.
The deepest wound…
wasn’t made by a weapon at all.”

Niveditaa thought for a while.

“Śakuni…”

“With the dice.”

Appaa nodded.

“Exactly — you got it right.
The Branches reach…
where the trunk never can.
That is why Vyāsa made Śakuni the branches.”

Appaa moved to the next line.

पुष्पफले

“Tell me…
what’s special about flowers and fruits?”

“Flowers are what everyone notices,”

said Neelakrishna.

Niveditaa thought for a moment.

“And fruits tell us…
what the tree has grown into.
They even carry the seeds…
for what comes next.”

Appaa nodded.

“Exactly.
Flowers and fruits tell us…
not how a tree begins…
but what it finally becomes.”

He looked at both of them.

“What was the moment…
when the whole world saw…
what the Kauravas had become?”

The children became quiet.

“Draupadī’s humiliation…”

said Niveditaa.

“And who laid hands on her?”

“Duḥśāsana,”

said Neelakrishna.

“Yes.
The humiliation of Draupadī…
was not where this tree began.
It was what the tree had finally become.
Like every fruit…
it carried within it…
the seeds of everything that followed.
That is why Vyāsa made Duḥśāsana…
its flowers and fruits.”

Finally, Appaa pointed to the last line.

मूलं राजा धृतराष्ट्रोऽमनीषी

“Tell me…
what’s special about roots?”

“They’re where the whole tree begins,”

said Neelakrishna.

Niveditaa imagined for a moment.

“And everything above…
draws its life…
from the roots.”

Appaa nodded.

“Exactly.
Everything above…
first drew its life…
from the roots.
That is why Vyāsa made Dhṛtarāṣṭra…
the root.”

Niveditaa looked again at the last word.

अमनीषी

“Appaa…
what does amanīṣī mean?”

Appaa pointed to the verse again.

“You remember?
Bhīṣma spoke.
Vidura spoke.
Kṛṣṇa spoke.”

“Dhṛtarāṣṭra understood every one of them.
Yet…
his mind would not go…
where his understanding led.”

“Vyāsa called such a man…
amanīṣī.”

The room became quiet again.

No one looked at the verse anymore.

The tree of Manyu stood before them.

After a while, Neelakrishna broke the silence.

“Appaa…
what about the Pāṇḍavas?”

“Yes…”

said Niveditaa.

“I’m sure…
Vyāsa didn’t leave us with just one tree.”

Appaa nodded.

“No…
he didn’t.”

“He showed us…
the tree of dharma too.”

Neelakrishna’s eyes lit up.

“Ah…
there’s another tree!”

Niveditaa smiled.

“Then…
there is another way to live.”

Appaa looked at both of them with quiet satisfaction.

“Yes.
That’s where we’ll begin tomorrow.”
