Ādi Parva · Episode 1 · Scene 2

Shaunaka's Āśrama

The Forest of Listening

Naimiṣāraṇya — The Forest of Listening

Neelakrishna had been waiting for Appaa to continue.

He was the first to break the silence.

"Appaa…
When Sauti reached Shaunaka's āśrama…
Did he tell them the story there?"

Before Appaa could answer, Niveditaa spoke.

"Wait.
Before that…
What was that place like?
Naimiṣāraṇya…
Shaunaka's āśrama…
What did it feel like?"

Appaa leaned back slightly.

"Naimiṣāraṇya was no ordinary forest.
It was an ancient forest of sages.
A place where yajña, tapas, study, and quiet conversation lived side by side."

Niveditaa listened intently.

Appaa continued.

"Imagine tall old trees.
Long shaded paths.
Āśramas hidden among the woods.
Sacred fires burning.
Smoke rising quietly through the evening air.
For long stretches, there would be silence.
And then—chanting.
Discussion.
Questions.
Debate."

He paused.

"There is also an old story about how that forest came to be known as Naimiṣāraṇya."

Niveditaa leaned a little closer.

Appaa continued.

"They say Brahmā once created a divine wheel.
A wheel born of thought.
He told the sages—
'Follow this wheel.
Where its rim breaks, that will be the place fit for tapas.'
The sages followed it.
Across lands.
Across forests.
At last, the wheel came to rest.
Its rim broke there.
And that sacred forest came to be known as Naimiṣāraṇya."

Neelakrishna was about to speak again.

"Wait," said Niveditaa.

She turned to Appaa.

"What about Shaunaka's āśrama?
And… who was Shaunaka?"

Appaa smiled.

"He was no ordinary ṛṣi.
He was a great sage.
A kulapati."

Neelakrishna asked,

"What is a kulapati?"

"A kulapati was not merely a teacher.
Many students lived under his care.
They studied there.
They performed tapas there.
He taught them, fed them, and took responsibility for their lives."

Appaa fell silent.

Niveditaa spoke softly.

"So…"

She paused.

"Shaunaka's āśrama was not just a hut in the forest.
It was almost like one large family of students…
Living, learning, growing together…"

Appaa paused for a moment.

Then he continued.

"After Sauti had rested, he spoke to the sages.
'Revered ones…
I stand before you now.
Ask what you wish to hear.
The sacred Purāṇas…
Stories of kings…
Stories of great ṛṣis…
Whatever your hearts seek.'"

Appaa's voice grew softer.

"The sages looked at one another.
Then they spoke.
With reverence.
'We wish to hear that sacred Bhārata of Vyāsa—
that holy Saṁhitā
which removes the fear born of adharma.'"

Silence returned to the room.

For a few moments, no one spoke.

Then Niveditaa looked at Appaa.

"Aah…"

She paused.

"So… even they began by asking to listen.
They too asked for the story."
