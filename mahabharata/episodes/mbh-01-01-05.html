Ādi Parva · Episode 1 · Scene 5

Vyāsa · Gaṇeśa

The Two Trees

The Two Trees

For a few moments, no one spoke.

Then Appaa continued.

“Sauti was now ready to begin the Mahābhārata.
But before he spoke of kings...
or battles...
or vows...
he first placed two images before the sages.”

Neelakrishna looked curious.

“Two images?”

Appaa nodded.

“Yes.
Not two people.
Two trees.”

The children listened quietly.

Appaa's voice became slower.

“Sauti said—

दुर्योधनो मन्युमयो महाद्रुमः
कर्णः स्कन्धः शकुनिस्तस्य शाखाः ।
दुःशासनः पुष्पफले समृद्धे
मूलं राजा धृतराष्ट्रोऽमनीषी ॥

युधिष्ठिरो धर्ममयो महाद्रुमः
स्कन्धोऽर्जुनो भीमसेनोऽस्य शाखाः ।
माद्रीसुतौ पुष्पफले समृद्धे
मूलं कृष्णो ब्रह्म च ब्राह्मणाश्च ॥”

Appaa looked at the children.

Then he softly repeated the verses once more.

“Duryodhano manyumayo mahādrumaḥ
Karṇaḥ skandhaḥ Śakunis tasya śākhāḥ।
Duḥśāsanaḥ puṣpa-phale samṛddhe
Mūlaṁ rājā Dhṛtarāṣṭro-'manīṣī॥

Yudhiṣṭhiro dharmamayo mahādrumaḥ
Skandho-'rjuno Bhīmaseno-'sya śākhāḥ।
Mādrīsutau puṣpa-phale samṛddhe
Mūlaṁ Kṛṣṇo brahma ca brāhmaṇāś ca॥”

Silence settled over the room.

Neither of the children spoke.

Niveditaa was the first.

“They don't feel like people...”

She searched for the words.

“They feel...
like two living worlds.”

Appaa looked at her and smiled.

Neelakrishna was still thinking.

“One tree...”

he said slowly,

“...grows from anger.”

He paused.

“And the other...”

“...from dharma.”

Appaa nodded.

“Yes.”

Nothing more was said.

The two trees remained quietly before them.

After a while, Appaa spoke again.

“And now...
Sauti began.”
