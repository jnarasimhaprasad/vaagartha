Ādi Parva · Episode 1 · Scene 3

Vyāsa · Gaṇeśa

The Mahābhārata Within

The Mahābhārata Within

Appaa continued.

"Sauti then began to tell the sages something remarkable.
'When Bhagavān Vyāsa had completed the Mahābhārata, a thought arose in his mind.
How should this great Itihāsa be preserved?
How should it reach generations yet to come?'"

Neelakrishna looked puzzled.

"Appaa...
If the Mahābhārata was already completed...
Why was Vyāsa worried?"

Appaa smiled.

"Because the Mahābhārata was complete in his mind.
But it had not yet been written down.
Verse after verse...
Thousands of them...
Each one had to be preserved exactly as he had composed it.
That was no small task."

Neither of the children spoke.

After a few moments, Niveditaa asked softly,

"The whole Mahābhārata...?
Already lived in his mind?"

Appaa nodded.

"Yes."

Neelakrishna slowly shook his head.

"How could anyone remember something so vast?"

Appaa remained silent.

Niveditaa was still thinking.

"So…"

She hesitated.

"The Mahābhārata was first here…"

She gently touched her forehead.

Appaa watched her quietly.

Then, with a gentle smile, he placed his palm upon his chest.

Niveditaa looked at him.

She smiled.

Only then did Appaa speak.

"Our tradition has a beautiful word.
Medhā."

The word rested quietly in the room.

"It is not simply memory.
It is that kind of intelligence by which what has once been truly known is never forgotten."

For a few moments, no one spoke.

Then Appaa continued.

"Sauti went on—
As Vyāsa reflected on how such a treasure should be preserved and shared, something unexpected happened.
Lord Brahmā himself came to his āśrama."

Neelakrishna's eyes widened.

"Appaa…
Brahmā came to Vālmīki too… didn't he?"

Appaa looked at him and smiled.

"Yes."

Neelakrishna nodded eagerly.

"I remember…
After Maharṣi Nārada came…
And after Vālmīki uttered his very first śloka…
Brahmā came."

Appaa nodded.

"Very good."

Niveditaa smiled.

"So Brahmā came to both Vālmīki and Vyāsa."

"He did," said Appaa.

Then he continued.

"Sauti said—
Vyāsa bowed before Lord Brahmā.
'Bhagavan,' he said,
'I have completed this Mahābhārata.
But I see no one on earth who can write it down.'
Lord Brahmā smiled.
'O Maharṣi...
Remember Gaṇapati.
He alone is worthy to be the scribe of this Mahābhārata.'
Having blessed Vyāsa,
Brahmā returned to his own abode."

The room was quiet.

Neelakrishna spoke first.

"Then...
Did Vyāsa remember Gaṇeśa?"

"He did," said Appaa.

Niveditaa leaned forward.

"And...
Did Gaṇeśa come?"

Appaa looked at both of them.

A gentle smile spread across his face.

"He did."

He paused.

"And what happened next..."

"I have always loved telling that part."
